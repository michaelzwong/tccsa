Mt. Huangshan

Who are we?

The TCCSA is a network for U of T Trinity College students of Chinese background and those who wish to learn more about Chinese culture.

Our Background

Rising prestige and international recognition of University of Toronto attracts a great influx of eager Chinese international students. As a result, the University of Toronto student body with Chinese heritage has become greater and greater. Yet despite the growing presence of Chinese students on campus, the expected increase in international communication and exchange seems to lag. More Chinese international students choose to stay and confine themselves within the comfort of homogenous campus groups and miss out on all other parts of University of Toronto, for example - Hart House, college life and campus resources such as CIE, Career Centre. To challenge the status quo and improve communication between all student bodies, TCCSA is born - We hope to build a bridge that not only improves accessibility of university and college events to Chinese students, but we also provide domestic students with gain in international perspective from experiencing the culture of China, a new giant in the international playground.

Our Mission

Trinity College Chinese Students Association was founded with the goal to involve not only students of Chinese Heritage, but all members of the Trinity College and University of Toronto Community. The ultimate mission is to build a channel for communication between the Chinese students and domestic students. To achieve such mission, we aim to achieve the following:

Firstly, we will build a support network for incoming students with Chinese heritage from Canada and abroad through events and peer mentorship. To achieve this, we have created a Chinese language social network based on “Wechat” (and also its facebook counterpart) that introduces and notifies students of social events and student government activities starting from their acceptance to Trinity College and University of Toronto.

Secondly, we will encourage Chinese students to participate and engage in Trinity college and University of Toronto events and student governance. In the past, participation of Chinese international students in college affairs has been meager.

Finally, we will and provide an opportunity for all students to learn and celebrate the Chinese culture. This goal will be achieved through our events. In the past we have successfully held two Lunar New Year High Tables in Trinity College (Guests are permitted), with attendances exceeding 200. Each academic year, we hold six to seven events on campus.
